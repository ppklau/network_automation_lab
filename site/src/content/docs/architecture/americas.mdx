## Why stub routers matter

The gap between what the SoT knows (full DC) and what the lab instantiates (stub) is intentional. It teaches a pattern you encounter in every real migration:

> The automation pipeline runs against the full SoT. Partial instantiation means some devices get pushed config that their neighbours can't yet validate end-to-end. The pipeline must handle that gracefully.

Batfish validates the *intent* — does the configuration, if fully deployed, satisfy the routing and security policies? It can do this even when only a fraction of the devices are live.
